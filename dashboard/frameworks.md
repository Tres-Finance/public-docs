# Frameworks Page

## Overview

The Frameworks page provides compliance and reporting tools to help organizations streamline their regulatory obligations. Currently featuring the **1099 Form** framework for tax reporting, the page will expand to include additional frameworks for audit preparation, SoDA compliance, MiCA regulations, and DORA requirements.

The Frameworks page enables you to:

- **Generate 1099 tax forms** for cryptocurrency transactions and payments
- **Automate compliance reporting** with guided workflows
- **Track framework completion** with progress indicators
- **Ensure regulatory compliance** for digital asset operations

## Available Frameworks

### 1. 1099 Form (Active)

**Status:** Available

**Description:** Reporting for Crypto Transactions: Compliance Made Simple

The 1099 Form framework guides you through generating IRS 1099 forms for cryptocurrency transactions, ensuring compliance with U.S. tax reporting requirements.

**Key Features:**

- Automated data collection from your TRES transaction data
- Support for multiple 1099 form types (1099-MISC, 1099-NEC)
- Progress tracking for each form generation

- Export completed forms for tax filing

**Available Form Types:**

- **1099-MISC**: Miscellaneous income reporting
- **1099-NEC**: Non-employee compensation reporting

**General Information Section:**
Each 1099 configuration displays organization details:

- Organization name
- Address
- State
- City
- ZIP Code
- Payers TIN (Tax Identification Number)

## 1099 Form Workflow

### Generating a New 1099 Form

To generate a new 1099 form:

1. **Start the Process:**

   - From the Frameworks page, click on the "1099 Form" card
   - Click "Start" to begin the 1099 generation flow

2. **Review General Information:**

   - The system displays your organization's general information:
     - Organization name
     - Address, State, City, ZIP Code
     - Payers TIN (Tax Identification Number)
   - Review and edit if necessary using the edit button

3. **Configure New Form:**

   - Click "Generate new flow" button
   - A configuration modal will appear

4. **Select Time Period:**

   - Click "Select Time Period for Calendar Year"
   - Choose the tax year for the 1099 form
   - For example: 2024, 2025, 2026

5. **Select 1099 Type:**

   - Click "Select 1099 Type"
   - Choose the appropriate form type:
     - **1099-MISC**: For miscellaneous income, royalties, rents, etc.
     - **1099-NEC**: For non-employee compensation (contractors, freelancers)

6. **Start Generation:**

   - Click "Start" to begin the data collection and processing
   - The system will analyze your transaction data for the selected time period
   - Processing progress will be displayed

7. **Monitor Progress:**
   - Return to the main page to see your new 1099 entry in the table
   - Progress is shown as X/3 (e.g., 1/3 = 33%, 2/3 = 67%, 3/3 = 100%)
   - Green checkmarks (✓) indicate completed steps

### Understanding Progress Indicators

The 1099 form generation consists of 3 main steps:

- **Step 1 (1/3)**: Data Collection - Gathering transaction data from your TRES account
- **Step 2 (2/3)**: Data Processing - Calculating and categorizing transactions
- **Step 3 (3/3)**: Form Completion - Finalized 1099 form ready for review and export

**Progress Visualization:**

- Progress bar shows completion percentage (33%, 67%, 100%)
- Step count shows current step (e.g., "1/3", "2/3", "3/3")
- Green checkmark indicates completed form (3/3)

### Managing Existing 1099 Forms

The 1099 Forms table displays all generated forms with the following information:

**Columns:**

- **Form type**: The 1099 form type (1099-MISC, 1099-NEC)
- **Year**: Tax year for the form
- **Progress**: Completion status and step count
- **Actions**: "Open" button to view form details

**Actions:**

- **Open**: View and access the generated 1099 form
- **Sort**: Click column headers to sort by Form type, Year, or Progress
- **Filter**: Use available filters to find specific forms (by year, form type)

### Completing a 1099 Form

When clicking "Open" on a completed form (3/3), you can:

- Review transaction data included in the form
- Edit recipient information if needed
- Export the completed 1099 form
- Generate PDF files for printing and filing
- Download data files for tax software import

## Common Workflows

### Generating Annual 1099 Forms

**Scenario:** Generate 1099-MISC forms for 2024 tax year

1. Navigate to Frameworks page
2. Click on "1099 Form" card
3. Click "Start"
4. Review your organization's general information
5. Click "Generate new flow"
6. Select Time Period: 2024
7. Select 1099 Type: 1099-MISC
8. Click "Start"
9. Monitor progress (wait for 3/3 completion)
10. Click "Open" when complete
11. Review and export the completed form

### Managing Multiple Form Types

**Scenario:** Generate both 1099-MISC and 1099-NEC forms for the same year

1. Generate 1099-MISC form first (follow steps above)
2. Wait for completion
3. Return to the 1099 Form page
4. Click "Generate new flow" again
5. Select the same Time Period (e.g., 2024)
6. Select 1099 Type: 1099-NEC
7. Click "Start"
8. Two separate forms will be generated for the same year with different form types

### Tracking Form Progress

**Scenario:** Check progress on multiple forms being generated

1. Navigate to the 1099 Form page
2. Review the table showing all forms
3. Check progress indicators:
   - Forms at 1/3 (33%) are still collecting data
   - Forms at 2/3 (67%) are processing data
   - Forms at 3/3 (100%) are ready to open
4. Click "Open" on completed forms to review and export
5. Check back later for forms still in progress

### Organizing Forms by Year

**Scenario:** Generate and manage forms for multiple tax years

1. Forms are automatically organized by tax year in the table
2. Sort by "Year" column to group forms chronologically
3. Each year can have multiple form types (1099-MISC, 1099-NEC)
4. Generate new forms for the current tax year as needed
5. Access historical forms for previous years for reference

## Tips

- **Plan Ahead:** Start generating 1099 forms well before tax filing deadlines to allow for review and corrections
- **Complete Data:** Ensure all transactions for the tax year are properly recorded in TRES before generating forms
- **Verify Information:** Double-check organization details (TIN, address) in the General Information section before generating multiple forms
- **Use Descriptive Tags:** Ensure transactions are properly tagged and categorized to improve accuracy of 1099 categorization
- **Review Before Filing:** Always review completed forms before exporting and filing with the IRS
- **Keep Records:** Download and securely store completed forms for your records
- **Generate Early:** Start the 1099 generation process early in the year to identify any data issues
- **Multiple Forms:** You can generate multiple form types for the same tax year for different types of income
- **Regular Monitoring:** Check progress regularly on forms being generated to ensure completion
- **Consult Tax Professional:** Always consult with a tax professional before filing 1099 forms to ensure compliance
- **Export Formats:** Completed forms can typically be exported in multiple formats (PDF, CSV, Excel) depending on your needs
- **Backup Data:** Keep secure backups of all exported 1099 forms for future reference and audits

## Important Notes

- **Tax Compliance:** Always consult with a qualified tax professional to ensure proper use of 1099 forms
- **Accurate Data:** Ensure all transaction data in TRES is accurate and complete before generating forms
- **Regulatory Requirements:** 1099 form requirements vary based on payment amounts and types—understand your obligations
- **Filing Deadlines:** Be aware of IRS filing deadlines (typically January 31 for 1099-NEC, February 28 or March 31 for 1099-MISC)
- **Recipient Copies:** Recipients must receive their 1099 forms by January 31
- **Form Type Selection:** Choose the correct 1099 type based on the nature of payments made
- **Data Privacy:** Ensure recipient information is handled securely and in compliance with privacy regulations
- **Amendment Process:** If corrections are needed, you may need to generate corrected 1099 forms
