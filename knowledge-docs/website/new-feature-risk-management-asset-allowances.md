# New Feature: Risk Management – Asset Allowances

Full visibility into your on-chain risk. In one place.

Token allowances are one of the most overlooked exposures in self-custodied wallets. Most teams have no easy way to see them all in one view, let alone quantify what’s at risk. TRES now brings every allowance across your org into a single page, with Asset Allowances – the first tab released as part of the new Risk Management module.

### Why Allowances Are a Hidden Risk

When a wallet interacts with an on-chain app, it grants an allowance – permission for a contract to move a specific token on its behalf. These don’t expire on their own. They stay active until revoked, which means old or unlimited allowances accumulate quietly across every wallet in an organization.

For a security or treasury team, this is a blind spot. There’s no native way to see every allowance a wallet has granted, no way to quantify the total exposure, and no way to flag the ones that matter most. The risk sits there, invisible, until something goes wrong.

### Full Visibility, Quantified

Asset Allowances brings every allowance into one place and calculates the exposure.

- Total value at risk: see how much could be lost if every spender pulled the maximum amount it’s allowed, with the most exposed allowances always sorted to the top.

- Unlimited allowances, flagged instantly: spot the top spenders and wallets driving the most risk across your org.

- Filter, group, and search: slice every allowance by wallet or asset to find exactly what you’re looking for.

- Full detail on demand: drill into each allowance to see what was approved, what’s been pulled, and what remains.

### Why This Matters Now

Self-custodied, EVM-based treasuries are exposed to this risk by default – funds, trading firms and market makers, DAOs and foundations, and exchanges and custodians all carry active allowances they likely haven’t reviewed in months. Asset Allowances gives these teams a controls layer that didn’t exist before: a single page to assess exposure and act on it.

This is the first release in a broader Risk Management module. Next up: a read-only dashboard scoring connected custodians against TRES Custody Security Guidelines, and Asset Risk Analysis, which quantifies how risky each asset in a portfolio is and how exposed that portfolio is as a result.
